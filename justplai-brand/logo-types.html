Don't refine the mark yet. First decide what kind of mark it should be.

There are seven canonical logo types. Each one would make Plai a different brand. Below, every type is rendered as a possible Plai logo so you can feel the range — not to pick a winner, but to widen the question. Then the last one is the push: the type a tool built on "make anything from pixels" is almost obligated to consider.

TYPE 08 — THE DYNAMIC / GENERATIVE MARK

What if the logo is never the same twice — because it's made in the product?

Here's the tension worth sitting in: Plai's entire promise is that anyone can make anything out of pixels. A single fixed mark quietly contradicts that. The truest expression of this product is a logo that is generated, drawn, and alive — a system, not a stamp. This isn't novelty: MIT Media Lab gave every person their own permutation; Nordkyn's mark breathes with the weather; Sagmeister let Casa da Música's logo recolor itself from its surroundings. For Plai, the move is even more native — the logo is something you draw, and everyone's is different. (This is also the through-line back to the "emergent UI" idea you started with.)

one mark · 40,000 permutations
A
The logo you draw
Onboarding ends by asking you to draw your own Plai mark. It becomes your avatar. The brand is literally co-made.
B
Never the same twice
A generative system (a seed + the palette + a grid) renders a fresh mark per screen, per user, per day. Recognizable as "Plai," unique every time.
C
The empty grid is the mark
The most radical: the logo is an empty canvas — an invitation. It fills with what the community makes. The brand is the work.
D
The mark is a verb
A single pixel being placed. The whole identity is the atomic act of creation, animated. "Plai" = the moment one block appears.

MIT Media Lab
An algorithmic logo with 40,000 permutations — every person claims their own. The canonical case for "a system, not a stamp."
Nordkyn (NEUE)
A "living identity" — the mark is generated from real-time weather data, so it reflects the place it represents.
Casa da Música (Sagmeister)
A logo generator that recolors itself from the imagery around each event. Infinite chromatic variation, one shape.
